The Swiss Clientele Challenge

Strategy · 01

The Swiss consumer's primary purchase decision is built on trust. Hiraya's brand is built on surprise. Managing the gap between these two is the entire marketing job. The menu must speak trust first — then earn permission to be surprising.

Lever 01

Retire the word "Fusion" — own the brand name

"Fusion" signals trendy concept to Swiss diners, activating skepticism rather than appetite. Swiss consumers eat at places that feel established, not experimental. Naming the category after the restaurant itself signals ownership, craft and permanence.

"Fusion Rolls" → "Hiraya Rolls"
Lever 02

Turn the raw fish problem into a selling point

A significant portion of conservative Swiss diners are curious about sushi but held back by raw fish. Currently this audience is invisible in the menu. Add a discreet cooking indicator to every item (R = roh, G = gegart, F = flambiert) and give the cooked-only option a proper story rather than hiding it in small print.

Add R · G · F indicators to all items
Lever 03

Speak German — consistently

The menu currently mixes English names, German descriptions, and Japanese terms without a clear logic. Swiss trust is partly linguistic: "Lachs" reads as craftsmanship; "Salmon" reads as imported marketing copy. Japanese sushi terms (Nigiri, Hosomaki, Temaki) are fine — they're trusted vocabulary. Everything else should be German-first.

"Salmon" → "Lachs" · "Shrimp" → "Crevetten" · "Tuna" → "Thun"
Lever 04

Tell one story per section — not a list

Swiss consumers trust expertise and curation. Each section should carry a short, confident intro that explains the philosophy — not just the contents. One or two sentences. Authoritative voice. First person ("unsere", "Gerwins Auswahl") rather than impersonal.

New intro copy for all 4 sections
Lever 05

Reorder sections to match the Swiss decision journey

Swiss diners scan for anchors before they explore. Starting with Starters gives the hesitant customer an easy first "yes" before committing to the full experience. The current order (Rolls first) throws them straight into the most unfamiliar territory.

New order: Starters → Hiraya Rolls → Sets → Poke & Bento

The One Insight That Changes Everything

Strategy · 02
"Hiraya should not market itself as a Filipino-Japanese fusion restaurant. It should market itself as the sushi restaurant that Gerwin built."

Swiss consumers trust people over concepts. Every premium artisan brand in Switzerland — bakers, butchers, winemakers — is built around a named individual. It is the oldest marketing playbook in the country, and it works precisely because it is rare in the restaurant category.

"Fusion" is a concept. Gerwin is a person. Every touchpoint — the website, the menu, the social — should carry a human signature. Not "we" or "the team": Gerwin. A specific palate, a specific point of view, a specific reason to come back.
