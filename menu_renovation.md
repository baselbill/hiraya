# Hiraya — Menu Renovation Plan

*Consultant recommendation · 2026-05-18*

---

## Diagnosis

The current Just Eat menu lists 62 items across 9 categories. It reads like a catalogue. The hosomaki and nigiri sections — 20 items — are pure commodity available at every sushi counter in Switzerland. They add operational complexity, dilute the brand, and tell customers nothing distinctive about Hiraya.

Hiraya's real differentiator is its fusion identity: Japanese technique, Filipino soul. The menu should lead with that and remove anything that doesn't reinforce it.

**The strategic principle:** the website is a brand showcase, not an order interface. Items that exist purely for completeness — individual maki pieces, niche nigiri, duplicate sets — belong on Just Eat, not on the website.

## What Happens to Hosomaki & Nigiri

Individual hosomaki and nigiri pieces are **removed from the website menu entirely**. They remain on Just Eat for customers who want to build their own order.

The website instead carries a brief callout in the menu section:

> *Looking for individual nigiri or hosomaki? Order directly on Just Eat — full selection available.*

This keeps the website focused on Hiraya's identity while not abandoning the revenue stream.
